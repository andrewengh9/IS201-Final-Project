Biking

These are some of the bike races I have completed in the past.

  1. True Grit
    • How long it has existed: the True Grit event has been around for many years and is one of the better-known endurance mountain bike events in southern Utah
    • Length: race distances vary, but longer versions are often in the 50-mile range
    • Time: depending on the course and rider level, it can take several hours, often around 4 to 7 hours for longer distances
  2. 12 Hours of Mesa Verde
    • How long it has existed: this endurance race has been a recognized event for years in the regional mountain biking scene
    • Length: riders complete as many laps as possible over a 12-hour period
    • Time: the full event lasts 12 hours, though individuals pace themselves differently depending on category and team setup
  3. Mountain ICUP races
    • How long they have existed: the Intermountain Cup series has been established for years and is a major race series in Utah cross-country mountain biking
    • Length: courses vary by venue and category, but races are generally cross-country style with multiple laps
    • Time: many races take around 1 to 2 hours depending on division, pace, and course difficulty
  4. NICA races
    • How long they have existed: NICA has been active since the 2000s and has become one of the biggest youth mountain biking organizations in the country
    • Length: race distances vary by category and course, usually designed for high school riders in cross-country formats
    • Time: many races are around 45 minutes to 2 hours depending on the division and number of laps

Hiking

A list of some of my favorite hikes.

  1. Mount Timpanogos
    • Elevation: about 11,750 feet
    • Time: roughly 8 to 10 hours round trip for many hikers
    • Location: American Fork Canyon area in northern Utah
    • Fun fact: Mount Timpanogos is one of the most iconic peaks in Utah and is especially known for wildflowers, mountain goats, and the glacier area near the summit
  2. Lone Peak
    • Elevation: about 11,253 feet
    • Time: roughly 9 to 11 hours round trip depending on route and pace
    • Location: Lone Peak Wilderness near Draper and Alpine, Utah
    • Fun fact: Lone Peak is known for its dramatic granite summit and huge views over the Wasatch Front
  3. Mount Pfeifferhorn
    • Elevation: about 11,326 feet
    • Time: roughly 7 to 9 hours round trip
    • Location: Little Cottonwood Canyon, Utah
    • Fun fact: Mount Pfeifferhorn is often considered one of the toughest and most respected Wasatch Front peaks because of its steep final push and exposed summit feel
  4. Twin Peaks
    • Elevation: about 11,330 feet
    • Time: roughly 7 to 9 hours round trip
    • Location: above Little Cottonwood Canyon near Salt Lake County, Utah
    • Fun fact: Twin Peaks gets its name from its double-summit appearance and gives an amazing high view over the central Wasatch
